Simple nutrition knowledge using Kenyan & East African foods

Eat Well, Live Healthy

Good nutrition helps prevent disease, improves energy, and supports growth. Learn how to eat well using affordable local foods available in Kenyan markets.

Carbohydrates Proteins Vegetables Fats Hydration Hygiene

Carbohydrates

Main Energy Foods

Carbohydrates give the body energy for work, school, and daily activities. They should make up 45-65% of your daily calories.

Local Examples:

Ugali Rice Chapati Matoke Cassava Sweet Potatoes
Tip: Choose whole grains over refined options. Brown rice and whole wheat chapati are healthier choices.
4 calories per gram
Long-lasting energy

Proteins

Body Building Foods

Proteins help build muscles, repair tissues, strengthen immunity, and produce enzymes and hormones. Essential for growth and development.

Local Examples:

Fish Beans Eggs Chicken Beef Lentils
Tip: Beans are affordable, healthy, and provide both protein and fiber. Mix with grains for complete protein.
4 calories per gram
Boosts immunity

Vegetables & Fruits

Protective Foods

These foods prevent diseases by providing essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. They support eye health, skin health, and overall immunity.

Local Examples:

Sukuma Wiki Spinach Cabbage Banana Mango Passion Fruit
Tip: Eat a rainbow of colors! Different colored fruits and vegetables provide different nutrients.
Rich in vitamins
Prevents diseases

Healthy Fats

Energy & Brain Support

Fats help absorb vitamins (A, D, E, K), support brain health, provide energy, and maintain healthy skin. Essential for hormone production.

Local Examples:

Avocado Coconut Oil Groundnuts Sunflower Oil Simsim Nuts
Tip: Use small amounts of healthy oils for cooking. Avoid reheating oil multiple times.
9 calories per gram
Insulates body

Water & Hydration

Essential for Life

Water helps digestion, blood circulation, temperature control, and removes waste from the body. Makes up about 60% of body weight.

Hydration Sources:

Clean Water Herbal Teas Fruits Soups Coconut Water
Tip: Drink at least 8 glasses (2 liters) daily. Carry a reusable water bottle and sip throughout the day.
60% of body
Detoxifies body

Food Hygiene & Safety

Disease Prevention

Proper food handling prevents diarrhea, worms, and food poisoning. Essential for keeping your family healthy and safe.

Good Practices:

Wash Hands Clean Surfaces Cook Thoroughly Store Properly Separate Raw/Cooked
Tip: Wash hands for 20 seconds with soap before handling food. Keep perishables refrigerated.
Prevents illness
Saves lives
